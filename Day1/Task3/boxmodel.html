Oasis

Oasis are an English rock band formed in Manchester in 1991. The group initially consisted of Liam Gallagher (lead vocals), Paul "Bonehead" Arthurs (guitar), Paul "Guigsy" McGuigan (bass guitar) and Tony McCarroll (drums). Liam asked his older brother Noel Gallagher (lead guitar, vocals) to join as a fifth member a few months later to finalise their formation. Noel became the de facto leader of the group and took over the songwriting duties for the band's first four albums.They are regarded as the most globally successful group of the Britpop era and one of the most influential rock bands of all time.

Oasis signed to the independent record label creation records in 1993 and released their record setting debut album Definitely Maybe (1994), which topped the UK Albums Chart and quickly became the fastest selling debut album in British history at that time.

In 1996, Oasis performed two nights as Knebworth for an audience of 125,00 each time, the largest outdoor concerts in UK history at that time.

In 1997, Oasis released their highly anticipated third album, Be Here, Now which became the fastest selling album in UK chart history selling 690,00 copies in its first week

In April 1995, "Some Might Say" became their first number-one UK single. At the same time, McCarroll was ousted from the band. He said he was unlawfully expelled from the partnership for what he called a "personal clash" with the brothers. The Gallaghers were critical of McCarroll's musical ability.

On 27 and 28 April 1996, oasis played their first headline outdoor concert at Maine Road football stadium, home of Manchester City FC, of whom the brothers had been fans since childhood.

Oasis played the final show of its first run on 22 August 2009 at V Festival at Weston Park. Two hours later Noel Gallagher announced he has left Oasis.
